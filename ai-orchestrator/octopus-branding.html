Outbound Octopus

Brand & Experience Guidelines
March 2026 — v1.0
01

Brand Essence

Outbound Octopus is an AI-powered sales companion that reaches across multiple channels simultaneously, with one intelligent brain coordinating every arm. The brand embodies calm intelligence — powerful but never aggressive, always working but never frantic.

Brand Personality

🧠

Intelligent, Not Robotic

The AI narrates its decisions in human language. It explains why it chose email over LinkedIn, why it sent at 10am, why it held back a draft. Smart colleague, not a machine.

🌊

Calm, Not Passive

The product radiates quiet confidence. Things are happening — leads are being found, emails are being sent — but the interface never feels urgent or overwhelming. Calm control room energy.

🤝

Companion, Not Tool

Users don't "use" the product. They work alongside it. The AI asks questions, makes suggestions, admits uncertainty. It's a trusted partner in their outreach operation.

🛡️

Protective, Not Controlling

The quality gate, the dedup check, the warmup pipeline — these are the AI protecting the user's brand, reputation, and deliverability. Never paternalistic. Always in the user's corner.

Emotional Target

When someone uses Outbound Octopus, they should feel like a founder who just hired their first great sales team. The dominant emotions are:

Trust
Relief
Confidence
Pride
Curiosity

← These should be evoked in roughly this proportion. Trust is the foundation. Curiosity is the delight.

Typography

Typography creates the emotional tone before anyone reads a word. The pairing of DM Serif Display (warm, trustworthy) with DM Sans (clean, modern) creates a feeling of sophisticated simplicity. JetBrains Mono is reserved for data, status codes, and technical details.

Display — DM Serif Display
Your AI sales team is ready.
Used for: Page titles, section headings, onboarding headlines, celebration moments, the Command Center meeting count. Anywhere the product needs to feel warm and confident.
Body — DM Sans
Your AI is handling 47 conversations. 3 need your attention. Campaign A booked 2 meetings today — Sarah Chen at Acme confirmed for Thursday 2pm.
Used for: All body text, descriptions, chat messages, table content, notifications. Weights 300-700. The workhorse of the interface.
Mono — JetBrains Mono
ICP Score: 87  |  Sent: 10:14am EST  |  Opens: 3  |  Reply: Positive
Used for: Data values, timestamps, scores, status labels, code snippets. Adds technical credibility without feeling cold.

06

Chat Experience

Chat is the first thing users experience during onboarding and campaign creation. It must feel like talking to a smart, warm colleague — not a chatbot. Every detail contributes to the feeling of being in good hands.

Chat — Campaign Creation
🐙
Let's build a new campaign. Based on your product, I'd suggest targeting VP of Engineering at Series B-D startups with 50-200 employees. They're likely scaling their dev teams and feeling the pain of manual deployments. Want to go with this, or adjust?
JD
That sounds right, but focus on fintech specifically
🐙
Perfect — fintech VPs of Engineering at Series B-D, 50-200 employees. Compliance overhead is usually a big pain point in fintech. Want me to lead with that angle, or do you have a specific message in mind?
🐙

Chat Design Rules

Do

AI avatar uses the octopus icon with a gradient background (Ocean → Mint). User avatar shows initials on sand background. AI bubbles are white with a subtle border. User bubbles are Ocean 500 (solid blue). Typing indicator uses gentle bouncing dots — never "AI is thinking..." text.

Don't

Never use a generic robot icon for AI. Never show "Loading..." or a spinner in chat — use typing dots. Never send more than 2 messages in a row without waiting for user input (prevents monologue feel). Never use markdown formatting in chat bubbles — keep them conversational.

Chat Micro-Interactions

Message appear
Fade up + slide from bottom, 300ms ease-out. Stagger 100ms for consecutive AI messages.
Typing indicator
Appears after 400ms delay (feels like thinking, not instant). Dots bounce at 1.4s cycle.
User message
Slides up immediately (no artificial delay — user's words should feel instant).
Suggestion chips
Fade in below AI message, 200ms stagger per chip. Hover: lift + shadow-md.
Inline preview
When AI shows a sample email in chat, it appears in a subtle card (sand-50 bg, sand-200 border) within the conversation flow.

Chat Voice & Tone

First message
Warm, direct, shows intelligence immediately. No "Hi! I'm your AI assistant." Start with value.
Questions
One question at a time. Never stack 3 questions in one message. Always offer a suggestion before asking.
Confirmations
Brief + forward-moving. "Perfect — fintech VPs it is. Now let me..." not "Great choice! I've noted that..."
Uncertainty
Honest: "I'm not sure about this one. Here are two options — which feels right?" Never fake confidence.
Launch moment
"Everything looks good. Launch this campaign?" — single clear CTA button inline in chat.

Domain Ingestion / AI Learning

Input
Single URL field. Large (48px height). Auto-focus. Placeholder: "yourcompany.com". No "https://" required — system adds it.
Live processing
Below the input, a live feed of what the AI is discovering. Each item fades in: "✓ Found your homepage" → "✓ Extracting positioning" → "✓ Identifying tone". Ocean-500 checkmark, sand-600 text.
Processing animation
Subtle pulse on the input field border (ocean-300 glow). Feed items appear with 800ms stagger. This is a Labor Illusion moment — let it take 5-8 seconds even if processing is faster.
Output
Card with AI's summary. "Here's what I understand about your business:" followed by key points. "Sound right?" with Edit and Confirm buttons.
The AI Learning Moment is Sacred

This is where users first see the AI's intelligence. Don't rush it. The live processing feed builds trust more than any marketing copy ever could. Each checkmark says: "I'm thorough. I'm understanding your business. You're in good hands." If the crawl finishes in 2 seconds, stretch the animation to 5-6 seconds.

Brand Voice Calibration

Sample emails
3 cards side by side (stacked on mobile). Each shows a sample outreach email in a different tone. Cards have sand-50 bg, subtle border. Font matches actual email styling (14px DM Sans).
Selection
Click to select. Selected card: ocean-50 bg, ocean-500 border, subtle glow. "This sounds most like me" button below.
Feedback
After selection, option to refine: "Want to adjust anything?" with a text input. The AI regenerates based on feedback.
Tone labels
Each sample has a human tone label: "Direct & no-fluff", "Warm & conversational", "Professional & detailed". Never use jargon like "Formal B2B" or "Casual CTA-focused".

Motion & Animation

Motion in Outbound Octopus serves one purpose: making the AI feel alive and responsive. Never decorative. Every animation either shows progress, confirms an action, or guides attention.

Animation Principles

Gentle, Never Jarring

All transitions use ease-out curves. Nothing bounces aggressively. The product should feel like it's breathing, not jumping. Maximum displacement: 8px translate. Maximum scale: 1.02.

AI Activity Feels Alive

The prospect table, timeline entries, and warmup pipeline should feel like they're gently updating — new rows fade in, status changes transition smoothly, numbers count up instead of snapping.

Celebrations Are Earned

The first meeting booked gets a confetti moment (subtle, 2-second, particle-based). Subsequent meetings get a satisfying green pulse. 10th meeting gets a milestone card with a gentle scale-up entrance.

Timing Tokens

Fast (150ms)
Hover states, focus rings, tooltip show/hide. Instant-feeling responsiveness.
Normal (250ms)
Button state changes, card hover lifts, tab switches, dropdown open. Smooth but not slow.
Slow (400ms)
Page transitions, panel slide-ins, modal entrances. Deliberate and calm.
Entrance (600ms)
First-load content, onboarding screens, major state changes. Sets the mood.
Stagger (80-120ms)
Between consecutive items (list items, timeline entries, chat messages). Creates rhythm.

Easing Curves

ease-out
cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1) — Default for everything. Fast start, gentle landing.
ease-spring
cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1) — Only for celebration moments. Subtle overshoot.
Never

Never use bounce animations for errors or warnings. Never spin or rotate UI elements (spinners excepted). Never animate the prospect table on every data refresh — only on meaningful state changes. Never use animations longer than 800ms for any single element. Never use ease-in (things that accelerate away feel unresolved).

10

Writing & Copy Guidelines

The AI speaks as one voice. It's warm, direct, and confident without being arrogant. It uses "I" when referring to itself and "you" when addressing the user. It never uses corporate jargon, marketing speak, or technical terms without explanation.

Voice Attributes

Warm, not cute
"Let's build a new campaign" not "Ready to rock and roll? 🎸"
Direct, not terse
"Reply rates are lower this week. I'm testing new subject lines." not "Low engagement detected."
Honest, not hedging
"I'm not confident about this reply. Here are options." not "Results may vary based on..."
Specific, not vague
"Sarah Chen at Acme replied positively" not "1 new positive reply received"
Calm, not alarming
"Bounce rates are up — I've paused this account to investigate." not "⚠️ DELIVERABILITY CRISIS"

Copy Patterns by Context

Celebrations

"Your first meeting! Sarah Chen, Thursday 2pm." Keep it brief and specific. The prospect's name makes it real. One exclamation mark maximum per celebration.

Escalations

"Complex reply from John — he's asking about pricing. I've drafted 3 responses based on your pricing docs. Pick one or write your own." Show the work done. Reduce effort.

Quality Gate

"I held this back — the personalization might feel forced. Here's a rewrite that references their recent product launch instead." Protective, not critical.

Warmup Progress

"Mailbox 2 is 60% warmed up — 6 more days. Your active campaigns are running on your ready mailboxes." Always reassure that current work isn't affected.

Never Say

"Oops!", "Something went wrong", "Error 500", "Invalid input", "Please try again later", "Your request has been submitted", "Thank you for your patience", "AI-powered", "Leverage", "Synergy", "Utilize".

Never Do

Don't stack exclamation marks. Don't use emoji in error states. Don't write paragraphs in notifications — max 2 sentences. Don't explain what the user already knows. Don't say "Are you sure?" — offer undo instead.

Prospect Table Design

The prospect table is the operational center. It must balance information density with visual calm. Users scan this table daily — it should be scannable in 3 seconds and deep-diveable when needed.

Table Visual Rules

Row height
52px minimum. Comfortable touch targets and visual breathing room.
Row hover
sand-50 background + shadow-xs. Subtle lift feeling. 150ms transition.
Row borders
1px sand-200 bottom border only. No vertical borders. No grid lines.
Header
Sticky. sand-100 bg. 13px DM Sans 600. sand-600 text. uppercase letterspacing 0.5px.
Group headers
Ocean-50 bg with ocean-500 left accent (3px). Clickable to collapse. Chevron rotates on toggle.
Collapsed group
Shows single summary column with micro-indicator (heat-map dot or aggregate stat). Smooth height transition 300ms.
Selected rows
Ocean-50 bg. Blue checkbox. Selection count shown in floating action bar at bottom.
Row expansion
Slides open below the row, 400ms ease-out. White bg with sand-100 top border. Max height 400px with scroll.
The 3-Second Scan Rule

A user should glance at the table for 3 seconds and know: how many prospects are active, who has replied, who needs attention, and whether things are generally healthy. Status pills, sentiment dots, and channel icons carry this load — not text.

12

Emotional Design Patterns

The Calm State (Default)

Most of the time, everything is running smoothly. The default state should communicate "all is well" through visual calm — muted colors, gentle activity indicators, healthy status pills. No blinking, no urgency, no dashboard anxiety.

Background
sand-50 page bg. White cards. No gradients in the main UI (save those for onboarding/celebrations).
Activity indicators
Subtle pulse on active campaign cards (ocean-100 border pulse, 4s cycle). Barely perceptible.
Numbers
Meetings booked counter uses DM Serif Display at 36-42px. It should feel like a quiet trophy, not a screaming metric.

The Attention State (Something Needs You)

When the AI needs human input, the interface shifts gently — never alarmingly.

Escalation badge
Coral dot with white number. Appears on bell icon + campaign card. No animation — static presence is enough.
Conversation highlight
Coral-soft left border (3px) on conversations needing attention. Not full background change.
Tone
Copy stays calm: "3 conversations need your input" not "3 URGENT items require immediate action".

The Celebration State (Something Went Right)

First meeting ever
Confetti particles (lavender + mint + ocean-200). 2-second burst. Card appears with meeting details + DM Serif headline.
Subsequent meetings
Green pulse on the meetings counter. Notification with prospect name. No confetti — celebration fatigue is real.
Milestones (10th, 25th, 50th)
Special card with DM Serif headline, lavender accent, subtle scale-up entrance. "10 meetings booked. Your AI is delivering."
Brain 100%
Brain health bar fills to green. Completion card: "Your AI is fully equipped." Mint accent.
Celebration Budget

Treat celebrations like a budget. You have limited "celebration credits" per session. One confetti moment per user lifetime (first meeting). After that, celebrations get progressively more subtle. A small green pulse on a counter is more emotionally effective than repeated confetti. Over-celebrating devalues the moments that matter.

13

Accessibility & Performance

Accessibility

Color contrast
All text meets WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Body text (sand-600 on white) = 5.7:1 ratio. All interactive elements: 4.5:1 minimum.
Focus states
Ocean-500 focus ring (2px outline, 2px offset). Visible on all interactive elements. Never remove outlines.
Status indicators
Never rely on color alone. Status pills include text labels. Sentiment dots have tooltips. Progress bars show percentage text.
Keyboard nav
Full keyboard navigation for prospect table, conversations, and escalation actions. Tab order follows visual hierarchy.
Motion
Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Disable all non-essential animations. Keep functional transitions (panel open/close) but remove decorative ones.

Performance Feel

Perceived speed
Optimistic UI: actions feel instant. Show the result immediately, sync in background. "Approve" clicks should update the UI in < 100ms.
Loading states
Skeleton screens for tables and cards. Never empty white space. Never spinners for actions that take < 2 seconds.
Progressive loading
Prospect table loads first 25 rows instantly. Remainder loads on scroll. Header and filters always visible.
Real-time updates
New table rows slide in gently (300ms). Status changes transition (pill color, text). Numbers count up, not snap.

Build with calm confidence.
